Welcome to the Kr'shna Brothers fan site.

Kr'shna Brothers were a Polish grunge band, which existed from 1993-1995. They were called "Polish Nirvana".

The first half of the 90s was a golden age in the history of rock - curly pseudo-metal musicians were washed away from the charts by a wave of great bands from Seattle, which revolutionized heavy guitar playing. Teens from all over the world have come to love Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden and Alice In Chains, and then charmed with their authenticity from local bands. In Poland, grunge (as the wave was called) was then represented by Hey and the Kr'shna Brothers.

HISTORY

Kr'shna Brothers in 1993

Kr'shna Brothers - a Polish band playing grunge music, existing in the years 1993-1995.

The beginnings of the band can be found at the end of 1992, when two young musicians from the hardcore group Dump (Adamczyk, Szydłowski) met with the experienced drummer of the Turbo band, Tomasz Goehs. Soon they were joined by another experienced musician, bassist Witold Urbański, known from the band Malarze i Żołnierze.

After several months of rehearsals, the band performed at the Jarocin festival, thanks to which they won a contract with Loud Out Records.

The group debuted with the album Food for Life, Spirit for Fuck. Among the songs that can be heard on this release, there is a cover of Marek Grechuta's song "Days, which we do not know". In addition, the album includes the song "We Know How to Kill", the riff of which was later used in the famous hit of the group Kazik Na Żywo "Artists". Apparently, this fragment cost Kazik "only" a case of beer.

Shortly after recording the band's second album "If", Loud Out Records went bankrupt. As a result, the already finished material was released in a negligible circulation only on cassette tapes and with no promotion at all. After some time, an offer from a large music label appeared, which was associated with the need to abandon other musical projects of all members of the group. As all the musicians had their shares in other groups, this contract was never signed.

In addition to the two studio albums mentioned, in 1994 Liveforfuck was released on the market. Another release is the Live album, i.e. a concert recording recorded at the Jarocin festival in 1993, together with the Alastor band, and released after the group broke up in 2001.

After the band ended, its vocalist sang in the Lizar formation. The musical style of this group was quite a surprise for the fans of the band. The drummer joined the group Kazik na Żywo, and Jarek Shadok Szydłowski joined the band Squot and Meggayogga. The bassist Witold Urbański, died in November 2008.

DISCOGRAPHY

Studio albums

Food For Life
Spirit For Fuck, 1993

Listen to this album

Food for Life, Spirit for Fuck - the debut album of Kr'shna Brothers, released in 1993 by Loud Out Records.

The riff from the composition "We know how to kill" was used (with the knowledge and consent of the creators) as the background for the song "Artists" by the group Kazik na Żywo, released on the album Live but in the studio (1994).

The song "Dni, whose Nie Znamy" is a cover of a composition by Marek Grechuta under the same title, originally published on the album Korowód (1971, music by Jan Kanty Pawluśkiewicz, lyrics by Marek Grechuta).

If, 1995

Listen to this album

Re-release of the second studio album of the band Kr'shna Brothers from 1995. The album is definitely more personal than the debut, which, however, due to an unfavorable coincidence, was released in a symbolic circulation and by many unnoticed at that time.

For many fans of the band, the re-edition is a great opportunity to get to know this hard-to-reach release. The album is available in a new remastered version in the digipak release.

Live albums

Live Jarocin, 1993

Listen to this album

The full live split includes episodes of the performances of the Polish grunge artists Kr'shna Brothers and the metal band Alastor at the rock festival in Jarocin.

This festival lasted from the 5th to the 7th of August 1993, with the figure of many groups and the complicity of almost twenty five thousand people. It was then that the Kr'shna Brothers team cut down several prizes, including a contract with Loud Out Records. Live recordings were released only eight years later (or five, after the group's disbandment) on MC in small circulation.
